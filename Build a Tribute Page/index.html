Abdul Sattar Edhi

"Angel of Mercy"

Tribute to Abdul Sattar Edhi

  • Full Name: Abdul Sattar Edhi
  • Born: January 1, 1928, in Bantva, Gujarat, British India
  • Died: July 8, 2016, in Karachi, Pakistan

Achievements and Contributions

  • Founder of Edhi Foundation, Pakistan’s largest and most respected welfare organization
  • Started his mission with a single ambulance and turned it into a nationwide network of 1800+ ambulances
  • Established free hospitals, clinics, orphanages, shelters, and rehabilitation centers across Pakistan
  • Known for serving humanity without discrimination of race, religion, or background
  • Collected and buried unclaimed bodies with dignity and respect
  • Provided homes to orphans and shelters to the homeless and mentally ill
  • Helped abandoned infants, encouraging people to leave them safely in “jhoolas” (cradles) placed outside Edhi centers
  • Saved thousands of lives during natural disasters, wars, and crises

Values and Personality

  • Lived a simple and humble life, wearing plain clothes and living in a small room
  • Believed in selfless service, honesty, and compassion
  • Never took a salary from the foundation
  • Refused foreign aid to keep his work independent and corruption-free

Recognitions

  • Awarded Nishan-e-Imtiaz, Pakistan’s highest civilian award
  • Nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize several times
  • Honored globally for his humanitarian work, including awards from the UN, BBC, and various international peace organizations
